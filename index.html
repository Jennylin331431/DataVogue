The environmental impact of fast fashion is undeniable, with the industry now responsible for 10% of the global annual carbon footprint. Beyond carbon emissions, fast fashion consumes vast amounts of natural resources, using 141 billion cubic meters of water annually and contributing to 35% of microplastics polluting our oceans.

The map on the left illustrates the global environmental impact of major fast fashion production countries. The data for this visualization comes from Sustainable Fashion, a Kaggle dataset that provides insights into waste production (in kilograms), water usage (in liters), and carbon footprint (in megatonnes) associated with clothing production across various material types. The dataset covers a time span from 2010 to 2024.

Which fashion materials cause the largest environmental impact?

The fashion industry is the second-largest industrial polluter. When factoring in the entire lifecycle of a garment, from manufacturing to transportation to, ultimately, ending up in landfill, in total, 1.2 billion tonnes of carbon emissions are released by the fashion industry every year.

Not only is the carbon footprint of the fashion industry influenced by the amount of waste sent to landfill, but CO₂ emissions during the manufacturing and transportation processes, as well as water pollution from dyeing processes and microfibre pollution, also contribute to the industry’s huge carbon and environmental footprint, highlighting the drastic need for change within the industry.

Solution: Slow Fashion
Each of us has the ability to make a direct impact by:
  • Reusing or purchasing secondhand clothing
  • Participating in clothing rentals
  • Advocating for a "wear more than once" mentality, rather than discarding items after a single use
